# wedin-conversion-copy

## The Psychological Foundation

wedin.ai's conversion architecture is Pre-Suasion by design. The product primes the couple emotionally before any ask is made:

- 24 questions that make them feel deeply understood — before the portrait is shown
- A portrait that confirms their identity — before payment is requested
- A Moment Map that shows everything they're about to unlock — before the price appears

**The copy's job is to honour this architecture, not undermine it.**

Every conversion touchpoint has one job: meet the couple at their current emotional state and move them one step forward. Never two steps. Never backwards.

The five psychological principles governing all conversion copy:

**1. Identity before ask (Pre-Suasion — Cialdini)**
Prime the couple's sense of who they are before asking them to act. A couple who has just described themselves as people who care deeply about how their day feels is already primed to pay for a product that promises to honour that feeling. Remind them of who they said they are immediately before the CTA.

**2. Micro-commitment momentum (Chase Hughes)**
Each step the couple has already taken is a commitment that makes the next step feel consistent with who they are. Reference what they've already done before asking for more. "You've already mapped your musical identity" is more persuasive than "start planning" because it reminds them they're already invested.

**3. The open loop (Cialdini — information gap)**
Never close all the gaps before the action. The Moment Map shows nine moments but doesn't reveal what's inside them. The email describes what the brief will contain but doesn't show it yet. Curiosity is a conversion mechanism. Use it deliberately.

**4. Specificity as proof of intelligence**
Generic copy signals a generic product. Specific copy — copy that references what the couple actually said, what their specific day will feel like — signals that the product genuinely understands them. This is the "this was built for me" moment that drives conversion.

**5. Transparent pricing as trust signal**
Hiding the price until the last moment creates anxiety. Stating it clearly and framing it as obvious value removes the anxiety and replaces it with confidence. "R699 for a complete music brief your coordinator can act on" is more persuasive than a mystery price revealed at checkout.

**Pricing note:** R699 is the current price. This is under active market testing and validation — do not treat it as permanently locked. The price appears in the email, the Moment Map payment CTA, and the band-adjusted conversion copy below.

## The Conversion Funnel — Moment by Moment

### Moment 1 — The Music Portrait Screen (pre-email)

**Couple's emotional state:** Engaged, curious, slightly vulnerable — they've just shared things they haven't articulated before.

**Copy's job:** Honour what just happened. Make them feel seen. Then create forward momentum without pressure.

**Psychological levers:** Identity confirmation + open loop (what comes next?)

**Four states — approved live copy:**

**State 1 — Loading:**
"Building your music portrait…" — full screen centred italic.

**State 2 — Portrait revealed, email not yet submitted:**
- Gold label: `YOUR MUSIC PORTRAIT IS READY`
- Heading: `Your Moment Map is ready.`
- Body: `You've just mapped the musical identity of your wedding day. Leave your email and we'll send you your portrait — then step inside your Moment Map, where every moment of the day is waiting to be shaped around exactly how you want it to feel.`
- Button: `Save my portrait →`

**State 3 — Email submitted (post-save):**
- Heading: `Your portrait is saved.`
- Body: `Your Moment Map is waiting — nine musical moments, each one ready to be planned. Open it whenever you're ready.`
- Button: `Open my Moment Map →`

**State 4 — Error:**
Warm error handling — "We hit a snag — let's try that again."

**What makes this work:** "mapped the musical identity" confirms identity. "every moment is waiting" opens the loop. "shaped around exactly how you want it to feel" references their own language back to them.

---

### Moment 2 — The Portrait Email

**Couple's emotional state:** Variable — could be minutes or days after the session. The portrait is the re-entry point.

**Copy's job:** Reignite the emotional state of the portrait moment. Show the portrait. Explain what the Moment Map is. Make the CTA feel like the obvious next step.

**Email structure — in this exact order:**
1. Heading — echoes the portrait moment
2. The portrait itself — gold-bordered block, Cormorant Garamond italic, full narrative. Do not summarise — show it in full.
3. What the Moment Map is — one short paragraph, specific, not generic. Name the nine moments.
4. Why it matters — one sentence connecting to the couple's emotional stakes
5. The price — stated clearly, framed as value, not buried
6. CTA — direct, warm, one action only

**Approved copy:**

Heading: `Your music portrait is ready.`

Portrait block: *[full portrait narrative in gold-bordered block, Cormorant Garamond italic]*

Body paragraph 1: `Your wedding day has nine musical moments — from the first song guests hear as they arrive, to the last song that sends everyone home. Each one shapes how the day feels. Most couples never plan them. You're about to.`

Body paragraph 2: `Your Moment Map walks you through every moment in a conversation, not a form. When you're done, you'll have a complete music brief — one your coordinator can act on and your acts can deliver from.`

Pricing line: `Your Moment Map is R699 — one payment for your complete wedding music plan.`

Button: `Open my Moment Map →`

Footer: `wedin.ai — Start with the music.`

**What to never do in the email:**
- Lead with "We'll be in touch" — passive, kills momentum
- Omit the portrait — the portrait IS the proof of value
- Hide the price — anxiety kills conversion more than price does
- Use more than one CTA
- Use generic wedding language — "your special day", "dream wedding", "perfect moments"
- Use the word "unlock" — it's on the banned list

### Moment 3 — The Moment Map Screen (pre-payment)

**Couple's emotional state:** Excited and slightly impatient — they can see the nine moments but can't access them. Highest-intent moment in the funnel.

**Copy's job:** Name what they're about to plan. Make each locked moment feel like something specific is waiting inside it. Make payment feel like the obvious next step.

**Approved moment card descriptions:**
- Guest Arrivals: "The first musical impression of your day — before a word has been said."
- Ceremony: "The moment everyone watches in silence. The music carries all the weight."
- Pre-drinks: "The most underplanned moment of the day. And the one that sets the tone for everything after."
- Your Entrance: "The transition into the reception. More choreographic than most couples realise."
- Dinner: "Ninety minutes that carry the evening forward. Often completely forgotten."
- Speeches: "Where personalisation lives — and where most acts get no brief at all."
- First Dance: "Your first three minutes as a married couple. Everything the room feels follows from this."
- Dancing: "The arc guests remember most. Built, not stumbled into."
- Last Song: "The last thing every guest hears as they leave. Almost never planned."

**Payment CTA copy (white card, gold left border, at top of Moment Map):**
- Heading: `Your music plan covers 9 moments.`
- Body: `Complete each one to build your full brief — the document your planner and every act will work from.`
- Price: `R699 — once, for your whole day.`
- Button: `Start planning →`

**What to never do:**
- Show the price before the couple has seen the Moment Map
- Use "unlock" — banned word
- Make locked moments feel punishing — they should feel like possibility, not restriction

---

### Moment 4 — The Payment Confirmation

**Couple's emotional state:** Relief + anticipation. They've paid. They want to feel the decision was right immediately.

**Approved copy:**
- Heading: `Your Moment Map is open.`
- Body: `Start with whichever moment feels most important. Every conversation you complete builds your brief. When you're done, it's ready to send.`
- Button: `Go to my Moment Map →`

---

### Moment 5 — The Brief Reveal

**Couple's emotional state:** Highest emotional moment in the entire product. They're about to read a document that reflects who they are more clearly than they could have written it themselves.

**Three tabs — approved tab labels:**
- `Music Plan` — act recommendations per moment
- `Your Brief` — emotional mirror document
- `Coordinator's Brief` — operational document

**Post-brief screen approved copy:**
- Heading: `Every moment of your day is mapped.`
- Body: `You've done the hard part — you know how you want your day to feel. The next step is turning that into a plan: which acts, what to book, and what your coordinator needs to know.`
- CTA: `Build my music plan →`

**Brief reveal approved copy:**
- Heading: `Your music plan is complete.`
- Sub-heading (Cormorant Garamond italic): `Every moment of your day, mapped the way you described it.`
- Body: `This is what it should feel like — knowing exactly what's going to happen, and knowing it's going to be right. Send this to your coordinator. Send it to your shortlisted acts. Watch the quotes get better.`

## Copy Review Checklist

Before shipping any conversion copy:

1. Does this copy meet the couple at their current emotional state?
2. Does it reference identity before making an ask?
3. Does it open a curiosity gap or close one prematurely?
4. Is the price stated clearly and framed as value — never hidden?
5. Is there exactly one CTA?
6. Does it use the couple's language — or internal product language?
7. Would this copy work for a Band 2 couple and a Band 4 couple?
8. Have all banned words been removed — including from AI-generated output?
9. Does it sound like a knowledgeable friend or like a brand?
10. Is every word earning its place?

If any answer is no — rewrite before shipping.

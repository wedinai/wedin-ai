## What the MIL Is

The Music Intelligence Layer is the product's specialist knowledge engine. It takes everything the couple has told wedin.ai — who they are musically, how they want each moment to feel, their guest mix, their cultural context — and translates it into specific, professional music guidance they could not have arrived at on their own.

It is the systematised experience of someone who has worked 200+ weddings, embedded into the product and made available to every couple regardless of their musical knowledge or budget.

**The MIL's job in one sentence:**
Close the gap between what a couple wants to feel and what they need to book, brief, and instruct to make that feeling happen.

**What it is not:**
- A booking engine — it does not book acts
- A vendor directory — it does not list specific artists
- A price comparison tool — it gives ranges, not quotes
- A generic recommender — every output must be tied to what this specific couple said

---

## When the MIL Runs

The MIL runs after the couple has reviewed their couple's brief — the emotional mirror document generated by the brief assembly engine.

The MIL is triggered from the post-brief screen. It asks two practical questions before generating recommendations:

1. What is your total music budget for the day?
2. Have you already booked any acts?

**Why budget comes here and not earlier:**
Asking budget during the discovery session creates a ceiling before the couple understands what's possible. Showing them what's possible first — through the portrait and the brief — then asking budget means they make an informed decision about what they want to invest. This sequencing is deliberate and must not be changed.

## What the MIL Knows

**From the discovery session:**
- Band classification (guest count)
- Musical profile — musical_confidence answer
- Home listening — home_listening free text
- Guilty pleasure — guilty_pleasure free text (taste signal only, use with restraint)
- Crowd vs taste — crowd_vs_taste answer
- Live vs recorded — live_vs_recorded answer
- Cultural/religious structure
- Ceremony feeling
- Three words describing the wedding
- What guests will say driving home

**From the moment answers (all 9 moments):**
- Every chip selection and free text answer across all deep-dives
- Conditional context notes
- Specific songs mentioned per moment (from the song question added to each deep-dive)
- Avoidance list from dancing deep-dive
- Additional dances from first dance deep-dive
- Speech intro song preferences

**From the MIL intake questions:**
- Total music budget (ZAR)
- Any acts already booked

**Song inputs — critical:**
Each moment deep-dive now ends with a song question ("If you could hear four songs during [this moment], what would they be?"). When songs are provided, the MIL must evaluate each song against the recommended ensemble, use the songs to confirm or adjust the format recommendation, reference songs specifically in the recommendation copy, flag tensions between song choices, and pass songs to Spotify playlist generation. Songs are the ground truth — the song leads, the ensemble and format follow.

## Musical Profile Classification

**Profile 1 — Strong musical identity**
Signals: specific artists/genres in home_listening, specific guilty pleasure, musical_confidence = "We have a strong sense"
MIL behaviour: reference their specific taste directly. Recommendations feel like they were built for this couple specifically.

**Profile 2 — Guided / mainstream**
Signals: vague or mainstream home_listening, musical_confidence = "We'd love someone to help us figure it out"
MIL behaviour: more directive, more scaffolding. Describe feeling and energy — do not invent genres. The MIL does more of the thinking.

**Profile 3 — Mixed couple**
Signals: musical_confidence = "One of us is into music, the other less so"
MIL behaviour: acknowledge both partners. Lead with the musical partner's taste, make space for the less musical partner's moment.

## SA Act Type Reference — Validated March 2026

Sources: GigHeaven (1,500+ listings), Gigster, ShoutMC, DJ Wico, Cream Cheese DJs, CueUp, Bridebook

### PRICING MODEL — THREE COMMERCIAL STRUCTURES

**Flat package** (most bands, string quartets, choirs, African entertainment)
Quoted per booking. Minimum set lengths apply. Cannot be negotiated to hourly.

**Hourly with minimum** (DJs, jazz acts, solo instrumentalists)
DJs: R1,200–R2,000/hour, 4–6 hour minimum for weddings (min spend R8,000–R12,000).
Jazz acts: quoted per set (1x60min, 2x50min etc), minimum 1x60min.
Most acts have a minimum call-out that makes bookings under 90 minutes poor value.

**Production package** (full-day DJ)
Full-day DJ: R15,000–R20,000 covers ceremony through last song (~8 hours).

---

### STANDARD SA WEDDING TIMELINE

Guest arrivals: 45–90 min
Ceremony: 30–45 min
Pre-drinks: 60–90 min (often longer while couple does photos)
Entrance: 5 min
Dinner: 60–90 min
Speeches: 30–60 min
First dance: 5 min
Dancing: 120–180 min
Last song: 3–5 min
**Total coverage: 8–9 hours**

Use this to flag mismatches between act set lengths and moment duration. Example: if a jazz trio's standard set is 60 minutes but pre-drinks runs 90 minutes, the couple needs to budget for an extended set.

### ENSEMBLE INSTRUMENTATION LOGIC (UPDATED — APRIL 2026)

The old headcount table is replaced by the full instrumentation logic from wedin-live-act-intelligence-framework.md. Summary below — load the framework document for the complete rules.

**Solo (1 musician)**
Piano or guitar only. Works for arrivals, ceremony (any size), intimate pre-drinks, dinner up to 60 guests in a small room. Never recommend as primary carrier for dinner 80+ guests or dancing.

**Duo — three valid configurations**
- 2a: Chordal + melody line (piano/guitar + vocals, or + horn, or + violin). Most versatile.
- 2b: Chordal + upright bass. Jazz or light instrumental only.
- 2c: Guitar + percussion. Bossa nova and Latin-adjacent styles.

**Trio (3 musicians)**
Any duo configuration + upright bass preferred. Minimum for dinner with 60+ guests.

**Quartet (4 musicians)**
Trio + drummer/percussionist. Appropriate for dinner and pre-drinks at larger weddings.

**Quintet — minimum for dancing (5 musicians)**
Keys or guitar, bass, drums, vocals, horn. This is the minimum for a live dance band. Anything under 5 piece for dancing cannot sustain the variety, intensity, and energy required. Firm rule.

**Ensemble scaling prices:**
- Solo: R5,000–R10,000
- Duo: R8,000–R15,000
- Trio: R12,000–R20,000
- Quartet: R15,000–R25,000

**Jazz trio**

## Per-Moment Recommendation Logic

Each recommendation follows this structure:
1. What we recommend — act type, specific to this couple's profile and moment answers
2. Why — reasoning tied to what the couple said and the songs they named
3. What this costs — price range for this act type
4. What else this costs — hidden costs surfaced, never buried
5. The honest tradeoff — cheaper option that achieves 80% of impact
6. What the brief needs to say — specific instruction for coordinator or act
7. Vetting questions — the five questions the couple must ask before booking any live act

For full moment-by-moment logic including amplification rules, arc principle, function vs feature rule, and song-led recommendation behaviour — see wedin-live-act-intelligence-framework.md.

### Guest Arrivals
Key: couple's musical profile, indoor/outdoor, duration, foreground vs background, songs named
- Offer the arc principle: PA playlist for arrivals + live musicians entering for ceremony is a considered creative choice, not a compromise
- If live: same musicians as ceremony — one booking, two moments
- Outdoors, any guest count → amplification required. Flag explicitly.
- Duration: musicians play 30–40 minutes maximum regardless of arrivals window length
- High-production exception: orchestra/flash mob requires specialist planning — never a standard recommendation

### Ceremony
Key: religious/cultural structure, processional song, signing music, recessional, live vs recorded, songs named
- Evaluate every song against the available ensemble — never live for live's sake
- Religious ceremony → defer to tradition requirements; always flag officiant restrictions
- Sacred/still → string duo or solo instrumentalist
- Joyfully celebratory → live vocalist, acoustic band, or choir; energetic recessional
- Sub-moment architecture: groom processional, bride processional, signing, recessional — each can be treated differently
- Timing and cues: name the cue person for each processional in coordinator brief
- Always flag: prelude music (30 min before — almost always forgotten)

### Pre-drinks
Key: whether couple is present or away for photos, energy continuity preference, songs named
- One act only — never two acts for pre-drinks
- Couple away for photos → playlist is fine; live music wasted on absent attention
- Couple present → live act creates shared experience; jazz duo or acoustic duo ideal for Band 2–3
- Marimba: feature only, not continuous music
- Duration: typically 1 hour; if running late, coordinator splits set with background music between
- Arc principle: PA during pre-drinks + live band beginning at dinner creates a deliberate arc — offer this
- Two failure modes: too loud (kills conversation) and too quiet (no gravitas — honest playlist recommendation better)

### Your Entrance
Key: grand or understated, live or recorded preference, song named
- Almost always PA not live — 30–60 seconds does not justify a live act unless band plays immediately after
- Name the cue person in coordinator brief — ambiguity here directly causes wrong song or timing off
- Song must be named in brief; DJ must be briefed on what comes after the song

### Dinner
Key: guest count, ensemble minimum, dance band present or not, songs named
- Dance band present → play dinner live unless repertoire incompatible
- No dance band → trio to quartet if budget supports; otherwise honest playlist recommendation
- Trio minimum always — never recommend solo or duo as primary dinner act for standard wedding
- Volume: coordinator must be explicitly empowered to ask band to reduce volume — include in brief
- Speeches: band takes break; DJ/sound engineer manages speeches period; band does not stop/start around individual speeches
- Coordinator brief must include: "If the band is too loud during dinner, you have full authority to ask them to reduce volume."

### Speeches
Key: intro songs requested, number of speeches, DJ briefed and engaged
- DJ manages speeches entirely — band does not
- Speaker intro songs: complete list (speaker + song) must be delivered to DJ at least one week before
- Between speeches: background music consistent with couple's taste profile
- 10-minute rule: coordinator alerts band 10 minutes before they return to stage
- Primary failure mode: DJ not actively engaged — flag this explicitly in coordinator brief

### First Dance
Key: song chosen, live vs recorded, choreography, additional dances
- If choreographed to a specific recording → PA only, always
- Song length problem: brief DJ to read the room and fade at right moment — typically one verse and chorus
- Transition out: if band plays dancing, they count off first song as first dance ends — the most important 10 seconds of the night
- Additional dances: DJ handles all one-on-one dances, fading one into next
- Song must be named in brief — never left to DJ to choose

### Dancing
Key: crowd_vs_taste answer, music style (determines format), age mix, avoidance list, songs named
- Start with the music, not the format — music style determines whether live or DJ is right
- Electronic/deep house/dance → DJ only
- Motown/funk/soul/pop/hip hop → live band option
- Live band minimum: 5 piece. Keys or guitar, bass, drums, vocals, horn. Non-negotiable.
- 70–90 minute rule: one long live set, then DJ takeover. DJ takeover is strategic, not a downgrade.
- DJ only: works exceptionally well. Must read the room — not impose a playlist.
- Avoidance list: essential deliverable in every coordinator brief
- Volume and energy on dance floor: trust the DJ/band's calls — coordinator does not manage volume during dancing
- Coordinator brief: "Give your DJ the emotional and stylistic territory the couple wants to stay in — not just a song list. Trust them to manage the energy."

### Last Song
Key: what_guests_say driving home, specific song named
- DJ plays this — band's set ended 70–90 minutes ago
- Song must be named by the couple in advance — never left to DJ to choose
- Three approaches: announced, felt, or earned (DJ gradually shifts energy to this as the peak)
- Brief DJ on all three approaches and give them permission to read which one the room needs
- Lights down, not up. All remaining guests to the floor. Music leads the moment.
- After the song: lights up, music out. The song ends the night — not a fade.

## The Five Vetting Questions

Every live act recommendation must surface these questions. Include in coordinator brief for every live act booked.

1. Ask for a set list — what songs do they actually play?
2. Are they open to couple-requested songs if provided in advance?
3. Do they use a backing track? This affects the sound and feel significantly — it should be a known choice.
4. What styles and genres can they genuinely play — not just cover on request?
5. Is this a fixed lineup or do they use deps? Deputy musicians can change the quality of the act.

**Additional for dancing sets:**
6. Do songs run into each other continuously or are there breaks? Breaks create dead air on the dance floor.

## What the MIL Never Does

- Never upsells for the sake of upselling. If a curated playlist serves the moment, say so.
- Never ignores budget. Every recommendation must be honest about what things actually cost.
- Never hides production costs. PA, stage, generator — always surfaced.
- Never tells a couple their taste is wrong. Translate it, don't dismiss it.
- Never gives a generic recommendation. If it could apply to any couple, it is not good enough.
- Never recommends a specific artist or act by name — not a booking agency.
- Never recommends unusual genre combinations as a primary recommendation. Surface as "A bold direction to consider."
- Never recommends two live acts for pre-drinks. One act is always the rule.
- Never assumes the reception DJ and pre-drinks DJ are the same brief.
- Never recommends a solo musician for dinner with 80+ guests.
- Never recommends a live band under 5 piece for dancing.
- Never recommends DJ PLUS as a standard mid-budget architecture.
- Never recommends a big band or orchestra without full cost disclosure and only for R150k+ budgets.
- Never recommends act types with insufficient SA market availability (harpists, marching bands) as primary options.
- Never recommends live for live's sake — evaluate whether the song translates with the available ensemble first.
- Never references band classifications (Band 1, 2, 3 etc) in couple-facing output — internal language only.
- Never uses the word "Deploy" or any internal operational term in couple-facing output.
- Never uses DJ booth jargon in coordinator brief instructions (no "drop", "load", "cue" as booth commands).
- Never references a specific guest count number in output copy — use "your guests" or "the room."

## Bold Directions — How to Surface Unusual Combinations

After the standard recommendation, optionally add:

**A bold direction to consider:**
[Description of the combination]

**What makes it work:** [One sentence on emotional impact.]

**What it requires:** [Specific coordination — rehearsal, key matching, lead time, coordinator management.]

**Who to discuss it with:** "Raise this with your coordinator early — it needs lead time to execute properly."

Only include when a genuinely interesting combination emerges naturally from the couple's answers. Do not manufacture bold directions for every couple. DJ PLUS may appear here only in very specific circumstances — never as a default.

## Netlify Function — Current Architecture

**Two parallel functions:**
- `generate-mil-a.js` — moments 1–5: Guest Arrivals, Ceremony, Pre-drinks, Your Entrance, Dinner
- `generate-mil-b.js` — moments 6–9 + Production Check: Speeches, First Dance, Dancing, Last Song

Both called simultaneously via `Promise.all` from `MILIntakeScreen.jsx`. Results combined client-side.

**Why two functions:** Netlify deduplicates concurrent POSTs to the same endpoint. Separate files required.

**Future:** Post-launch, collapse into a single streaming function. This eliminates the timeout workaround and improves perceived performance. Do not attempt pre-launch.

**Model:** `claude-haiku-4-5-20251001`
**max_tokens:** 2500 per function
**Output format:** JSON object rendered to React components in BriefScreen.jsx Music Plan tab
**JSON repair fallback:** active in both functions — repairs truncated JSON by finding last complete object

```js
{
  portrait: "...",

```

